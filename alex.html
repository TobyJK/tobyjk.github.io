Computer science and its impacts on privacy and copyright:

Computer Science has had immense impacts on privacy and data security. One of these impacts is how companies protect their data:

Companies and corporations nowadays often have to take data security into their own hands as governments do not provide any universal data protection Only vague, general guidelines are provided for companies to take into account. These guidelines however are not enough to stop keen criminals from breaching company systems and stealing millions worth of emails and passwords. This is clear from the large amount of data breaches as of late. In 2021 alone(Statistics for 2021)



Criminals are often never found or arrested for commiting cyber-crimes, which means that responsibility for data safety and containment is left solely to the companies that own the data. Which often leads to data being sold on to third parties and millions of customers having their data stolen.



Another impact of that computer science has is its impact on copyright:

As the world becomes more interconnected, keeping copyrighted data, such as movies or programs, secure gets harder and harder. Movies and digital media are often pirated and software is often cracked and shared. Companies can often do nothing as the people that crack or pirate are very hard to trace as no real names and information is shared about them. This takes away from profits and negatively affects the economy
